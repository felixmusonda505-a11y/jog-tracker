How offline/online actually works conceptually: an app that "works offline and online" isn't magic — it just means the app stores data on the phone itself (so it has something to show with no internet) and talks to the internet when available to update that data. So your app needs two things: (1) local storage on the phone for whatever data it needs even with no signal, and (2) a way to check if internet is available and fetch/sync fresh data when it is. In App Inventor this maps directly to two components: TinyDB (stores data locally on the device — works with zero internet) and a Web component or CloudDB (talks to the internet when it's available). You'd build a block of logic roughly like: "if internet is connected, fetch new data and save it to TinyDB; otherwise just load whatever's already in TinyDB." That's the entire offline/online pattern in plain terms — every offline-capable app, no matter how complex, is a variation of that idea.
